Cream was rock's first supergroup: Eric Clapton (guitar), Jack Bruce (bass/vocals), and Ginger Baker (drums). In just two and a half years, they pioneered blues-rock, power trios, extended improvisation, and arena-level volume. Their influence on hard rock, metal, and jam bands is immeasurable.

Early Life

Formed in London in 1966 when Eric Clapton, already known as "God" from his work with John Mayall's Bluesbreakers and the Yardbirds, teamed up with bassist Jack Bruce and drummer Ginger Baker. All three were considered the best players on their respective instruments in Britain.

Career & Music

Fresh Cream (1966) announced their arrival. Disraeli Gears (1967) produced Sunshine of Your Love. Wheels of Fire (1968) was the first platinum-selling double album. Their live shows featured extended improvisations lasting 20 minutes or more. But ego clashes — particularly between Bruce and Baker — tore them apart.

Did you know?

Eric Clapton was so revered before Cream that fans spray-painted "Clapton is God" on the walls of London. When he saw the graffiti, he was embarrassed rather than flattered. Baker and Bruce fought so viciously that Baker once chased Bruce around the studio with a knife.

Legacy

They lasted barely two years but changed rock forever. Sunshine of Your Love, White Room, and Crossroads remain cornerstones of rock guitar. They proved that rock musicians could be virtuosos and that blues could be played at ear-splitting volume. Every power trio since owes them a debt.
