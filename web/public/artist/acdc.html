AC/DC have been delivering no-frills, high-energy hard rock for over fifty years. The Young brothers — Angus and Malcolm — built the band on thunderous riffs, pounding rhythms, and an unshakable commitment to the raw power of rock and roll. Back in Black is the second-best-selling album in history.

Early Life

Formed in Sydney, Australia in 1973 by Scottish-born brothers Malcolm and Angus Young. Angus's schoolboy uniform stage outfit became iconic. With Bon Scott's raspy vocals, they became Australia's biggest rock export.

Career & Music

Highway to Hell (1979) was their breakthrough. When Bon Scott died in February 1980, the band recruited Brian Johnson and recorded Back in Black as a tribute, selling over 50 million copies. They maintained their signature sound across decades, never chasing trends.

Did you know?

The opening riff of "Thunderstruck" took Angus Young so long to perfect that his fingers bled during recording. The band's name was inspired by the letters "AC/DC" on a sewing machine belonging to Malcolm and Angus's sister Margaret.

Legacy

They have sold over 200 million records. Back in Black is the best-selling hard rock album ever. Their refusal to change their formula proved that pure, unadulterated rock and roll never goes out of style. Angus Young's guitar tone is one of the most recognizable sounds in music.
