Anna Mae Bullock, known as Tina Turner, was one of the most electrifying performers in music history. After enduring years of abuse during her partnership with Ike Turner, her solo comeback with Private Dancer (1984) became one of the greatest redemption stories ever told, selling over 200 million records and earning her the title "Queen of Rock 'n' Roll."

Early Life

Born on November 26, 1939, in Brownsville, Tennessee. She grew up picking cotton and singing in the church choir. At 18, she met Ike Turner at a nightclub in St. Louis and joined his band. They married in 1962, but the marriage was marked by severe domestic violence. She endured 16 years of abuse before leaving Ike in 1976 with nothing but her name.

Career & Music

After leaving Ike, Tina spent years performing in small venues. Her comeback began with a cover of Al Green's "Let's Stay Together" (1983). Private Dancer (1984) sold 20 million copies and won four Grammy Awards. "What's Love Got to Do with It" became her signature hit. She became the first solo female artist to fill stadiums, and her farewell tours broke attendance records worldwide.

Did you know?

When Tina left Ike in 1976, she had 36 cents and a Mobil gas card to her name. She took only her stage name in the divorce settlement. Eight years later, Private Dancer made her one of the biggest stars in the world, proving her resilience was as extraordinary as her voice.

Legacy

Tina Turner sold over 200 million records and won 12 Grammy Awards including a Lifetime Achievement Award. She was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame twice — with Ike Turner in 1991 and as a solo artist in 2021. Her story of survival and triumph inspired millions. She died on May 24, 2023, at age 83 in Switzerland.
