Michael Joseph Jackson is widely regarded as the most influential entertainer of all time. From his childhood stardom with the Jackson 5 to the stratospheric success of Thriller — the best-selling album in history — he redefined what a pop artist could be, merging music, dance, fashion, and visual storytelling into a singular art form.

Early Life

Born on August 29, 1958, in Gary, Indiana, the eighth of ten children. His father Joseph drilled the Jackson brothers relentlessly, forming the Jackson 5. Michael became the group's lead singer at age six, captivating audiences with his extraordinary vocal ability and natural stage presence. The Jackson 5 signed with Motown in 1969 and immediately scored four consecutive No. 1 hits.

Career & Music

Off the Wall (1979) established Michael as a solo superstar. Thriller (1982) shattered every record — 70 million copies sold, seven Top 10 singles, eight Grammy Awards. The music videos for Thriller, Billie Jean, and Beat It transformed MTV and the music video art form. Bad (1987) and Dangerous (1991) continued his dominance. His moonwalk debut on Motown 25 in 1983 became the most famous dance move in history.

Did you know?

The Thriller music video, directed by John Landis, cost $500,000 to make — an unheard-of sum in 1983 — and runs 13 minutes and 42 seconds. It was the first music video inducted into the National Film Registry by the Library of Congress.

Legacy

Michael Jackson sold over 400 million records worldwide and won 13 Grammy Awards. He pioneered the modern music video, broke racial barriers on MTV, invented iconic dance moves, and proved that pop music could be high art. His influence extends across every genre and every generation of performers that followed.
