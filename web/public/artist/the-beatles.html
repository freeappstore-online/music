The Beatles are the most influential and commercially successful band in the history of popular music. John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison, and Ringo Starr transformed rock and roll from teenage entertainment into an art form, selling over 600 million records worldwide.

Early Life

Formed in Liverpool in 1960, the band honed their craft playing marathon sets in the clubs of Hamburg, Germany. Manager Brian Epstein polished their image and secured a recording contract with EMI's Parlophone label. Producer George Martin became their indispensable collaborator.

Career & Music

From 1963's Please Please Me to 1970's Let It Be, they released 13 studio albums in just seven years. Beatlemania swept the world. They evolved from pop singles to the studio experimentation of Rubber Soul, Revolver, and Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band, then to the raw brilliance of the White Album and Abbey Road.

Did you know?

The Beatles hold the record for the most No. 1 hits on the Billboard Hot 100 with 20. When they appeared on the Ed Sullivan Show on February 9, 1964, crime rates among juveniles reportedly dropped to zero during the broadcast.

Legacy

They hold the record for most No. 1 albums on the UK charts and most No. 1 hits on the Billboard Hot 100. They pioneered the concept album, music videos, stadium concerts, and artistic independence for recording artists. Their breakup in 1970 marked the end of an era.
