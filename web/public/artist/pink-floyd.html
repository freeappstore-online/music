Pink Floyd created some of the most ambitious and sonically innovative music in rock history. Their conceptual albums exploring alienation, time, madness, and war, combined with pioneering use of studio technology and spectacular live shows, made them one of the best-selling and most critically acclaimed bands of all time.

Early Life

Formed in London in 1965 by Syd Barrett, Roger Waters, Richard Wright, and Nick Mason. Barrett's whimsical psychedelia drove their early sound, but his deteriorating mental health led to his departure in 1968. David Gilmour replaced him, and the band evolved into something entirely new.

Career & Music

The Dark Side of the Moon (1973) spent 937 weeks on the Billboard charts. Wish You Were Here (1975) was a tribute to Barrett. The Wall (1979) became a rock opera and film. After Waters departed in 1985, Gilmour led the band through further successful albums and tours.

Did you know?

The Dark Side of the Moon stayed on the Billboard 200 chart for 937 weeks — nearly 18 years. The album's iconic prism cover has become one of the most recognizable images in popular culture, despite containing no text or band name.

Legacy

They sold over 250 million records worldwide. The Dark Side of the Moon is one of the best-selling albums in history. Their influence extends beyond music into visual art, philosophy, and the very concept of the album as an art form. The Wall remains one of rock's most powerful statements about isolation.
