For over 50 years, B.B. King's expressive guitar playing and warm vocals defined modern blues. His guitar "Lucille" and his signature vibrato became the sound of the blues worldwide.

Early Life

Born Riley B. King on a cotton plantation in Mississippi. Sang gospel in church as a boy. Moved to Memphis in 1948 where he got a radio job and the name "Blues Boy," shortened to B.B.

Career & Music

Recorded The Thrill Is Gone in 1969, his biggest hit. Toured relentlessly — over 300 shows a year for decades. Influenced every blues and rock guitarist who followed.

Did you know?

B.B. King named every one of his guitars "Lucille" after a 1949 incident where two men fighting over a woman named Lucille knocked over a kerosene heater, setting the venue on fire. King ran back into the burning building to save his guitar.

Legacy

Played 15,000+ concerts over his career. Won 15 Grammy Awards. The Thrill Is Gone became the most recognizable blues song in the world. Died in Las Vegas at 89.
