Freddie Mercury, born Farrokh Bulsara, was the lead vocalist and songwriter of Queen, widely regarded as one of the greatest rock singers of all time. His four-octave vocal range, flamboyant stage persona, and extraordinary showmanship redefined what a rock frontman could be. Bohemian Rhapsody, the six-minute operatic rock epic, remains one of the most inventive songs ever recorded.

Early Life

Born on September 5, 1946, in Stone Town, Zanzibar (now Tanzania), to Parsi-Indian parents. He attended boarding school in India where he formed his first band, the Hectics, and where schoolmates gave him the nickname "Freddie." He moved to England in 1964 and studied graphic art and design at Ealing Art College in London.

Career & Music

Mercury formed Queen in 1970 with Brian May, Roger Taylor, and John Deacon. The band broke through with "Killer Queen" (1974) and reached global fame with "Bohemian Rhapsody" (1975), which spent nine weeks at No. 1 in the UK. Queen's performance at Live Aid on July 13, 1985, before 72,000 at Wembley Stadium and 1.9 billion TV viewers, is widely considered the greatest live rock performance in history.

Did you know?

Freddie Mercury had four extra teeth in his upper jaw (mesiodens), which gave him his distinctive overbite. He refused to have them removed, believing they contributed to his extraordinary vocal range. Scientists later confirmed his voice had a unique vibrato and subharmonic quality found in no other recorded singer.

Legacy

Freddie Mercury died of AIDS-related bronchopneumonia on November 24, 1991, at age 45. His death brought unprecedented attention to the AIDS crisis. Queen has sold over 300 million records worldwide. "Bohemian Rhapsody" has been streamed over 2 billion times. The 2018 biopic of his life became the highest-grossing music biography film ever.
