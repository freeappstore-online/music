Sir Elton Hercules John is one of the most commercially successful and enduring artists in music history. His four-decade partnership with lyricist Bernie Taupin produced some of the most beloved songs in popular music. With over 300 million records sold, flamboyant stage costumes, and extraordinary piano skills, he became one of the defining figures of 1970s rock.

Early Life

Born Reginald Kenneth Dwight on March 25, 1947, in Pinner, Middlesex, England. He began playing piano at age three and won a scholarship to the Royal Academy of Music at 11. In 1967, he answered a newspaper ad from Liberty Records and was paired with lyricist Bernie Taupin — the beginning of one of songwriting's greatest partnerships.

Career & Music

His self-titled album (1970) and "Your Song" established him as a major talent. The early-to-mid 1970s brought a staggering run: Honky Chateau, Goodbye Yellow Brick Road, Captain Fantastic — each a multiplatinum hit. His flamboyant costumes, oversized glasses, and explosive live shows made him the biggest rock star of the decade. "Candle in the Wind 1997," his tribute to Princess Diana, became the best-selling single of all time.

Did you know?

At the height of his fame in the 1970s, Elton owned over 250,000 pairs of glasses. He once estimated his monthly flower bill at $400,000. His wardrobe is so extensive that the Victoria and Albert Museum has exhibited his costumes.

Legacy

Elton John has sold over 300 million records, won five Grammy Awards, an Academy Award, and a Tony Award. He was knighted by Queen Elizabeth II in 1998. His Farewell Yellow Brick Road tour (2018-2023) became the highest-grossing concert tour in history. His AIDS foundation has raised over $600 million.
