Deep Purple, alongside Black Sabbath and Led Zeppelin, formed the "unholy trinity" that created heavy metal. Ritchie Blackmore's neoclassical guitar, Jon Lord's Hammond organ, Ian Gillan's screaming vocals, Roger Glover's bass, and Ian Paice's drumming produced some of the heaviest and most technically accomplished rock of the 1970s.

Early Life

Formed in Hertfordshire, England in 1968. The original Mark I lineup blended psychedelic rock with classical music, culminating in the Concerto for Group and Orchestra performed at the Royal Albert Hall with the Royal Philharmonic Orchestra in 1969.

Career & Music

The Mark II lineup (Blackmore, Lord, Gillan, Glover, Paice) produced the classic albums In Rock (1970), Fireball (1971), Machine Head (1972), and Made in Japan (1972). Smoke on the Water became the most recognizable guitar riff in rock history. Internal tensions led to numerous lineup changes.

Did you know?

The riff to Smoke on the Water was inspired by a real fire. While recording Machine Head at a casino in Montreux, Switzerland, a fan fired a flare gun at the ceiling during a Frank Zappa concert in the same venue, burning the entire building down. The band watched the fire from across the lake and wrote the song about it.

Legacy

The Guinness Book of World Records once listed them as the world's loudest band. They were inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2016. Smoke on the Water's opening riff is usually the first thing every beginning guitarist learns. They have sold over 100 million records.
