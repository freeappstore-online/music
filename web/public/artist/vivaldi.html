Vivaldi was a virtuoso violinist and prolific composer who wrote over 500 concertos and 46 operas. The Four Seasons remains one of the most popular pieces of classical music worldwide.

Early Life

Born in Venice in 1678. Ordained as a priest (his red hair earned him the nickname "Il Prete Rosso") but was excused from saying Mass due to chest ailments — likely asthma.

Career & Music

Spent most of his career at the Ospedale della Pietà, a girls' orphanage in Venice, where he taught and composed. His concertos influenced Bach, who transcribed several of them.

Key Works

Did you know?

Bach so admired Vivaldi that he transcribed at least 10 of his concertos for other instruments. Without Vivaldi, Bach's music might sound very different.

Legacy

Died in poverty in Vienna in 1741 and was largely forgotten for 200 years. Rediscovered in the 1920s, his music sparked a Baroque revival.
