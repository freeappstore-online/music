"The spiritual seeker who turned the saxophone into a prayer"

Coltrane's technical mastery and spiritual searching pushed jazz into new territory. A Love Supreme is considered sacred in jazz. His "sheets of sound" technique influenced every saxophonist who followed.

Early Life

Born in Hamlet, North Carolina. Both grandfathers were ministers. Lost his father, aunt, and grandparents all within a year at age 12. Took up saxophone in high school.

Career & Music

Played with Miles Davis and Thelonious Monk before leading his own groups. A Love Supreme (1964) fused jazz with spiritual devotion. His late work pushed into free jazz and Eastern-influenced improvisation.

Key Works

Did you know?

Coltrane practiced so obsessively he would fall asleep with the saxophone still in his mouth. He once said, "I want to be a saint."

Legacy

Died of liver cancer at just 40. In his short career, he transformed jazz more profoundly than almost anyone except Louis Armstrong. A Love Supreme was entered into the Library of Congress.
