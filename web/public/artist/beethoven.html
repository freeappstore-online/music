Beethoven bridged the Classical and Romantic periods, expanding every form with unprecedented emotional power. He composed many of his greatest works while profoundly deaf.

Early Life

Born in Bonn in 1770. His father pushed him relentlessly hoping to create another Mozart. By 11 he was deputy court organist. Moved to Vienna in 1792 to study with Haydn.

Career & Music

His "Heroic" period produced the Eroica, Fifth Symphony, Emperor Concerto, and Fidelio. As hearing deteriorated, his music became more experimental. Late works include the Ninth Symphony and late string quartets.

Did you know?

At the Ninth Symphony premiere, Beethoven was so deaf a soloist had to turn him around to see the standing ovation.

Legacy

An estimated 20,000 attended his funeral. He transformed the symphony from entertainment into profound expression. The Ninth's "Ode to Joy" became the Anthem of Europe.
