Marvin Pentz Gay Jr. (he added the "e" to his surname) was one of the most important and influential artists in American popular music. He began as a smooth Motown hit-maker and evolved into a visionary artist whose What's Going On is widely regarded as one of the greatest albums ever made — a soulful meditation on war, poverty, ecology, and spirituality.

Early Life

Born on April 2, 1939, in Washington, D.C. His father was a church minister who frequently beat him. Marvin found solace in music, singing in his father's church choir and teaching himself piano, drums, and guitar. After a stint in the Air Force, he moved to Detroit and auditioned for Berry Gordy's Motown Records.

Career & Music

His early Motown hits — "How Sweet It Is," "I Heard It Through the Grapevine," and duets with Tammi Terrell — made him one of the label's biggest stars. But What's Going On (1971) transformed him from pop singer to artist. He fought Motown to release it; Berry Gordy called it "the worst thing I've ever heard." It became Motown's fastest-selling single and the album is now considered a masterpiece. Let's Get It On (1973) and "Sexual Healing" (1982) showcased his sensual side.

Did you know?

Berry Gordy initially refused to release "What's Going On," calling it "the worst thing I've ever heard." Marvin threatened to never record for Motown again. Gordy relented — the single became Motown's fastest-selling ever, and the album is now universally regarded as one of the greatest of all time.

Legacy

Marvin Gaye was shot and killed by his father on April 1, 1984, one day before his 45th birthday. His legacy as an artist who proved that pop music could address serious social issues while remaining profoundly beautiful is unmatched. What's Going On consistently ranks among the greatest albums in music history.
