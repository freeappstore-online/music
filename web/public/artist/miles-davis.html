Miles Davis shaped the course of modern jazz across five decades, pioneering cool jazz, hard bop, modal jazz, and fusion. Kind of Blue is the best-selling jazz album of all time.

Early Life

Born in Alton, Illinois to a middle-class family. His father was a dentist. Started playing trumpet at 13. Moved to New York at 18, ostensibly to study at Juilliard but really to find Charlie Parker.

Career & Music

Led legendary quintets and sextets. Kind of Blue (1959) pioneered modal jazz. In a Silent Way and Bitches Brew (1969-70) invented jazz fusion. Constantly reinvented his sound.

Did you know?

Miles once told a White House dinner guest who asked what he did for a living: "I've changed the course of music five or six times. What have you done?"

Legacy

Died in 1991. Every phase of his career — cool, hard bop, modal, fusion, electric — became a movement. He influenced not just jazz but rock, hip-hop, and electronic music.
