Queen married the power of hard rock with operatic ambition, theatrical showmanship, and pop brilliance. Freddie Mercury's four-octave vocal range, Brian May's orchestral guitar layers, John Deacon's melodic bass, and Roger Taylor's powerful drumming created a sound that was grandiose yet accessible.

Early Life

Formed in London in 1970 by Brian May and Roger Taylor with Freddie Mercury (born Farrokh Bulsara in Zanzibar) and later John Deacon. May built his own guitar, the Red Special, with his father from a Victorian fireplace mantle.

Career & Music

Bohemian Rhapsody (1975) defied every rule of pop music — a six-minute single with an operatic section — and became one of the most iconic songs ever recorded. We Will Rock You and We Are the Champions became the ultimate stadium anthems. Their Live Aid performance at Wembley in 1985 is considered the greatest live rock performance in history.

Did you know?

Brian May built his iconic Red Special guitar with his father from a 100-year-old fireplace mantel, motorcycle valve springs, and a knitting needle for the tremolo arm. He still plays it at every concert, over 50 years later.

Legacy

Mercury died of AIDS-related pneumonia in 1991, but Queen's music has only grown in stature. The 2018 film Bohemian Rhapsody introduced them to a new generation. They have sold over 300 million records. "We Will Rock You" is played at virtually every sporting event on Earth.
