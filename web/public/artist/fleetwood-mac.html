Fleetwood Mac is the rare band whose greatest album was born from personal catastrophe. Rumours, recorded while its members were breaking up with each other, channeled raw heartbreak into perfect pop-rock songs. The result sold over 40 million copies and became one of the defining albums of the 1970s.

Early Life

Founded in London in 1967 by Peter Green, Mick Fleetwood, and John McVie as a British blues band. After Green's departure, the band went through numerous lineup changes before recruiting the California duo Lindsey Buckingham and Stevie Nicks in late 1974.

Career & Music

The Buckingham-Nicks lineup's self-titled 1975 album was a hit, but Rumours (1977) was a phenomenon — fueled by three couples in various stages of romantic collapse. Go Your Own Way, Dreams, The Chain, and Don't Stop became massive singles. Tusk (1979) was a daring double-album experiment.

Did you know?

During the recording of Rumours, John and Christine McVie were divorcing, Lindsey Buckingham and Stevie Nicks were splitting up, and Mick Fleetwood discovered his wife was having an affair. They channeled all that pain into perfect pop songs — and cocaine bills reportedly exceeded the recording budget.

Legacy

Rumours has sold over 40 million copies, making it one of the best-selling albums in history. Stevie Nicks became rock's most iconic woman. Dreams went viral on TikTok in 2020, introducing the band to yet another generation. Their story proves that great art often comes from great pain.
