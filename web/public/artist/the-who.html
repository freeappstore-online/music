The Who were one of the most explosive and innovative bands in rock history. Pete Townshend's windmill guitar chords, Roger Daltrey's powerful vocals, John Entwistle's thundering bass, and Keith Moon's manic drumming created a wall of sound that influenced punk, power pop, and arena rock.

Early Life

Formed in London's Shepherd's Bush in 1964 from the ashes of the Detours. Under the management of Kit Lambert and Chris Stamp, they became leaders of the Mod movement, playing furious rhythm and blues in West London clubs.

Career & Music

My Generation (1965) became a youth anthem. Tommy (1969) was rock's first fully realized opera. Who's Next (1971) featured the synthesizer-driven Baba O'Riley and Won't Get Fooled Again. Quadrophenia (1973) told the story of a Mod named Jimmy in 1960s Brighton.

Did you know?

Keith Moon was so destructive that hotels banned the band worldwide. He once drove a Lincoln Continental into a Holiday Inn swimming pool on his 21st birthday. The Who were banned from every Holiday Inn in the world.

Legacy

Keith Moon died in 1978 and John Entwistle in 2002, but their music endures. They pioneered instrument destruction on stage, the rock opera, the use of synthesizers in rock, and maximum-volume performance. They were inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1990.
