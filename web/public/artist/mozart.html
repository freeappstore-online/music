A child prodigy who began composing at five, Mozart produced an astonishing body of work across every genre — symphonies, concertos, operas, chamber music — all marked by melodic beauty and emotional depth.

Early Life

Born in Salzburg, showed extraordinary talent from age three. His father Leopold took him on European tours where the child prodigy astonished royal courts. By twelve, he had written his first opera.

Career & Music

Moved to Vienna in 1781 as a freelance composer. Composed The Marriage of Figaro, Don Giovanni, The Magic Flute, his final symphonies, piano concertos, and the unfinished Requiem.

Did you know?

At 14, Mozart transcribed Allegri's Miserere — a Vatican secret — from memory after a single hearing.

Legacy

Died at 35 leaving 800+ compositions. His music embodies the Classical ideal while hinting at Romanticism. Beethoven, Schubert, and countless others built upon his innovations.
