Black Sabbath created heavy metal. Tony Iommi's downtuned, crushing guitar riffs, Ozzy Osbourne's eerie vocals, Geezer Butler's heavy bass lines, and Bill Ward's thunderous drumming forged a dark, heavy sound that spawned an entire genre and influenced every metal band that followed.

Early Life

Formed in Birmingham, England in 1968 as the Polka Tulk Blues Band, then Earth. The industrial city's factory-town atmosphere seeped into their sound. Tony Iommi lost the tips of two fingers in a factory accident, forcing him to downtune his guitar — inadvertently creating the heavy metal sound.

Career & Music

Their self-titled debut (1970) and Paranoid (1970) established the template for heavy metal. Iron Man, War Pigs, and Paranoid became defining songs. Master of Reality (1971) went even heavier. The band went through numerous lineup changes after Osbourne's departure in 1979.

Did you know?

Tony Iommi lost the tips of his middle and ring fingers on his right (fretting) hand in a sheet metal factory accident on his last day of work. He fashioned prosthetic fingertips from melted plastic bottles and detuned his guitar to reduce string tension — accidentally inventing the heavy, dark sound of metal.

Legacy

They reunited with the original lineup for The End tour in 2017. They are recognized as the creators of heavy metal by virtually every musician and critic. Without Black Sabbath, there would be no Metallica, no Slayer, no doom metal, no stoner rock — no heavy metal at all.
