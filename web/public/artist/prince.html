Prince Rogers Nelson was one of the most gifted and prolific musicians in history. A virtuoso multi-instrumentalist, singer, songwriter, and producer, he played all 27 instruments on his debut album at age 19. His fusion of funk, rock, R&B, pop, and new wave created a sound that was entirely his own.

Early Life

Born on June 7, 1958, in Minneapolis, Minnesota. Named after his father's jazz group, the Prince Rogers Trio. He taught himself to play piano at age seven, guitar at 13, and drums at 14. By his teens he was writing, arranging, producing, and performing all instruments on his own recordings. He signed with Warner Bros. at 17.

Career & Music

His early albums established his genre-blending approach, but 1999 (1982) and Purple Rain (1984) made him a global superstar. Purple Rain sold over 25 million copies and spawned the No. 1 hits "When Doves Cry" and "Let's Go Crazy." He wrote hits for others including "Nothing Compares 2 U" for Sinead O'Connor and "Manic Monday" for The Bangles. His famous name change to an unpronounceable symbol in 1993 was a protest against his record label.

Did you know?

At his 2004 Rock and Roll Hall of Fame induction, Prince performed a legendary guitar solo on "While My Guitar Gently Weeps" that many consider the greatest live guitar solo ever captured on camera. He tossed his guitar into the air at the end — it was never seen coming down.

Legacy

Prince sold over 150 million records worldwide and won seven Grammy Awards, an Academy Award, and a Golden Globe. He proved that a single artist could master every aspect of music creation. His vault reportedly contains thousands of unreleased recordings. His Paisley Park compound in Minneapolis is now a museum.
