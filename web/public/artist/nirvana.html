Nirvana brought underground punk and alternative rock crashing into the mainstream. Kurt Cobain's anguished songwriting, raw vocals, and ability to write unforgettable melodies made Nevermind a cultural earthquake. They proved that authenticity could sell millions.

Early Life

Formed in Aberdeen, Washington in 1987 by Kurt Cobain and Krist Novoselic. After several drummers, Dave Grohl joined in 1990, completing the classic lineup. Their debut Bleach was recorded for just $606.

Career & Music

Nevermind (1991) knocked Michael Jackson off the top of the Billboard charts. Smells Like Teen Spirit became a generational anthem. In Utero (1993) was a deliberate retreat from commercial polish. MTV Unplugged in New York (1994) revealed Cobain's acoustic brilliance and emotional depth.

Did you know?

Bleach was recorded for just $606.17. Within two years, Nevermind had sold over 10 million copies. The baby on the Nevermind cover was paid $200 for the shoot; the photograph has since become one of the most iconic album covers in history.

Legacy

Kurt Cobain died on April 5, 1994, at age 27. Nirvana had existed for just seven years and released only three studio albums, but they fundamentally changed the direction of popular music. Grunge replaced glam metal, and authenticity replaced spectacle. Grohl went on to form the Foo Fighters.
