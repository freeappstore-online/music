Eagles blended rock, country, and folk into a smooth, meticulously crafted sound that became the soundtrack of 1970s California. Hotel California is one of the most recognizable recordings in music history, and Their Greatest Hits (1971-1975) was the first album to be certified 38x platinum in the United States.

Early Life

Formed in Los Angeles in 1971 as Linda Ronstadt's backing band. Glenn Frey, Don Henley, Bernie Leadon, and Randy Meisner struck out on their own, signing to Asylum Records. Their blend of country harmonies and rock muscle was instantly appealing.

Career & Music

Take It Easy (1972) launched them. Desperado (1973) established their outlaw mythology. Hotel California (1976) was a masterpiece about the dark side of the American Dream. The Long Run (1979) was another hit. Internal tensions led to a bitter breakup in 1980, followed by a hugely successful reunion in 1994.

Did you know?

When asked about a potential reunion after their bitter 1980 breakup, Don Henley famously said it would happen "when hell freezes over." In 1994, they titled their reunion album and tour Hell Freezes Over. Their Greatest Hits has sold over 38 million copies in the US alone.

Legacy

Their Greatest Hits is the best-selling album in US history. Hotel California's guitar solo is the most famous in rock. Despite famously contentious relationships within the band, they created music of enduring beauty. Glenn Frey's death in 2016 was mourned across the music world.
