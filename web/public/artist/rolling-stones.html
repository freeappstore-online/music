The Rolling Stones are the definitive rock and roll band. Where the Beatles expanded the boundaries of pop, the Stones stayed true to the raw, rebellious spirit of blues-based rock. Mick Jagger and Keith Richards have been rock's greatest partnership for over sixty years.

Early Life

Mick Jagger and Keith Richards grew up in Dartford, Kent, reconnecting on a train platform in 1961 over a shared love of blues. With Brian Jones, Bill Wyman, and Charlie Watts, they formed a band devoted to the music of Muddy Waters and Chuck Berry.

Career & Music

From the mid-1960s through the early 1970s, they produced an unmatched run of classics: (I Can't Get No) Satisfaction, Paint It Black, Sympathy for the Devil, Gimme Shelter, and Brown Sugar. Albums like Beggars Banquet, Let It Bleed, Sticky Fingers, and Exile on Main St. defined rock and roll.

Did you know?

Keith Richards's riff for "(I Can't Get No) Satisfaction" came to him in his sleep. He recorded the melody on a bedside tape recorder and went back to sleep. The next morning, the tape contained two minutes of guitar followed by 40 minutes of snoring.

Legacy

They have sold over 240 million records. Their lips-and-tongue logo is one of the most recognized symbols in popular culture. Charlie Watts's death in 2021 was mourned worldwide, but the band continues to tour and record, proving that rock and roll truly never dies.
