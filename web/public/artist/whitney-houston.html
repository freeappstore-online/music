Whitney Elizabeth Houston possessed one of the most extraordinary vocal instruments in the history of popular music. Her combination of power, range, control, and emotional depth set a standard that has never been matched. She sold over 200 million records worldwide and remains the only artist to chart seven consecutive No. 1 Billboard Hot 100 singles.

Early Life

Born on August 9, 1963, in Newark, New Jersey. Her mother was gospel singer Cissy Houston, her cousin was Dionne Warwick, and her godmother was Aretha Franklin. She began singing in church as a child and by her teens was a sought-after backup vocalist and model. Clive Davis signed her to Arista Records in 1983.

Career & Music

Her self-titled debut album (1985) produced three No. 1 hits and became the best-selling debut by a female artist. Whitney (1987) entered the Billboard 200 at No. 1 — a first for a female artist. Her rendition of "I Will Always Love You" from The Bodyguard soundtrack (1992) became one of the best-selling singles of all time. She starred in several films and won six Grammy Awards.

Did you know?

Whitney's rendition of "The Star-Spangled Banner" at Super Bowl XXV in 1991, performed during the Gulf War, was so popular it was released as a single and reached No. 20 on the Billboard Hot 100 — the only time the national anthem has charted as a pop single.

Legacy

Whitney Houston redefined vocal excellence in pop music. Her influence can be heard in every major female vocalist who followed — from Mariah Carey to Beyonce to Adele. Her rendition of "The Star-Spangled Banner" at Super Bowl XXV (1991) is considered the greatest performance of the national anthem in history.
