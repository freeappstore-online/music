The Clash were punk's most ambitious and politically engaged band. While the Sex Pistols burned bright and fast, the Clash evolved, incorporating reggae, ska, rockabilly, hip-hop, and world music into their furious punk foundation. London Calling is routinely cited as one of the greatest albums ever made.

Early Life

Formed in London in 1976 by Joe Strummer (who left the 101ers), Mick Jones, Paul Simonon, and Terry Chimes (later replaced by Topper Headon). They emerged from the same scene as the Sex Pistols but had broader musical ambitions from the start.

Career & Music

Their self-titled debut (1977) was raw punk fury. Give 'Em Enough Rope (1978) was produced by Sandy Pearlman. London Calling (1979) was a genre-smashing double album. Sandinista! (1980) was a sprawling triple album. Combat Rock (1982) brought mainstream success with Rock the Casbah and Should I Stay or Should I Go.

Did you know?

The iconic cover of London Calling shows Paul Simonon smashing his bass guitar on stage at the Palladium in New York. Photographer Pennie Smith initially didn't want to use it because she thought it was out of focus. It went on to become the most famous photograph in punk rock history.

Legacy

Joe Strummer died in 2002, but the Clash's influence is immeasurable. They proved punk could be musically sophisticated without losing its political edge. London Calling's cover — Simonon smashing his bass — is the most iconic photograph in punk history.
