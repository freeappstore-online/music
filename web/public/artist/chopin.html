Chopin composed almost exclusively for solo piano, creating nocturnes, études, preludes, waltzes, mazurkas, and polonaises of unmatched poetic beauty and technical innovation.

Early Life

Born near Warsaw in 1810. A child prodigy, he gave his first public concert at age 7 and published his first composition at 8. Studied at the Warsaw Conservatory.

Career & Music

Left Poland at 20, never to return. Settled in Paris where he became the most sought-after piano teacher. His relationship with writer George Sand lasted nine years. He composed 230 works, all featuring the piano.

Did you know?

Chopin requested that his heart be removed after death and sent to Poland. It rests in a pillar of the Holy Cross Church in Warsaw to this day.

Legacy

Died of tuberculosis at 39. His music redefined what the piano could express. Liszt, Debussy, Rachmaninoff, and every pianist since has been influenced by his innovations.
